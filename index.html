Drone A Whale

A Citizen Science Whale Photogrammetry Project

Help us study whale health by sharing your drone footage. Your videos help scientists measure whale body condition and track population health. Every contribution advances whale conservation.

About the Project

Drone A Whale collects aerial footage of whales from the public and uses photogrammetry to measure whale body condition. These measurements help us understand whale health and track populations over time.

Who Runs This Project

This project is led by Associate Professor Rebecca Dunlop at the University of Queensland, along with her research lab team, with help from Haneen Abu-Obeid, PhD candidate at UQ.

What is Citizen Science?

Citizen science is when members of the public contribute to scientific research. By sharing your whale footage, you're helping us understand whale health and conservation needs.

How your contribution helps:

  • Track whale population health over time
  • Identify and monitor individual whales
  • Understand pregnancy and breeding success
  • Study environmental impacts on whales
  • Inform conservation strategies and policies
  • Cover broader geographic areas than researchers alone

What is Photogrammetry for Whales?

Photogrammetry lets us take precise measurements from drone footage. By filming whales from directly above, we can measure their body length, width, and volume to assess their health without disturbing them.

These measurements tell us if whales are healthy, undernourished, or pregnant. Good quality footage and accurate metadata are essential for reliable measurements.

What We Need From You

  • Overhead drone footage of whales (filmed from directly above)
  • Clear footage with minimal water glare
  • A shareable link to the footage
  • Metadata about filming conditions in the form below

Privacy & Research Use

All footage is used strictly for research purposes only. Your videos will be analyzed solely for scientific study of whale body condition and health.

Your submissions remain completely anonymous unless you choose to provide your contact details in the optional section. We do not share footage with third parties outside of research purposes.

How to Share Your Footage

Upload your video using one of the methods below and paste the shareable link in the form. Make sure the link allows viewer access.

Option 1: Google Drive

▼
  1. Go to drive.google.com and sign in to your Google account
  2. Click the "+ New" button (top left) and select "File upload"
  3. Select your drone footage video file from your computer and wait for it to upload completely
  4. Once uploaded, find the file in your Drive, right-click on it, and select "Share"
  5. In the sharing settings, click "Change to anyone with the link" under "General access"
  6. Ensure the permission is set to "Viewer"
  7. Click "Copy link" at the bottom of the dialog
  8. Paste the copied link in the metadata form below

Option 2: YouTube (Unlisted)

▼
  1. Go to youtube.com and sign in to your account
  2. Click the camera icon (top right) and select "Upload video"
  3. Select your drone footage file and wait for it to upload
  4. Set the visibility to "Unlisted" (not Public or Private)
  5. Add a title if desired, then click "Next" through the remaining steps
  6. Once published, click "Share" and copy the video URL
  7. Paste the link in the metadata form below

Option 3: File Transfer Services

▼

WeTransfer

  1. Go to wetransfer.com (no account needed for free version)
  2. Click "Add your files" and select your drone footage
  3. Choose "Get transfer link" option
  4. Click "Transfer" to start uploading
  5. Once complete, click "Copy link"
  6. Paste the link in the metadata form below
  7. Note: Free WeTransfer links expire after 7 days. For permanent storage, consider Google Drive or Dropbox

Dropbox

  1. Sign in to dropbox.com
  2. Click "Upload files" and select your video
  3. Once uploaded, hover over the file and click "Share"
  4. Click "Create link", then "Copy link"
  5. Paste the link in the metadata form below

Microsoft OneDrive

  1. Sign in to onedrive.com
  2. Click "Upload" and select your video file
  3. Right-click the uploaded file and select "Share"
  4. Click "Copy link" (ensure it's set to "Anyone with the link")
  5. Paste the link in the metadata form below
